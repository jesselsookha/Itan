# Itan

> *From the Yoruba tradition — story, history, narrative.*  
> *The accumulated account of a life.*

---

**Itan** is an open portfolio development framework for students and emerging
professionals across all disciplines.

It is not a template. It does not tell you what your portfolio should look like.

It builds the thinking behind one — from first principles, for your discipline,
at your stage, for the audiences that matter to you.

---

## The Live Guide

The full Itan guide is published at:

**[jesselsookha.github.io/Itan](https://jesselsookha.github.io/Itan/)**

Read it there. It is structured for navigation, not for browsing raw files.

## What Itan Covers

| Part | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Ⅰ — Foundations | What a portfolio actually is, the five stages, and how it relates to your CV and platforms |
| Ⅱ — Identity | Your narrative anchor, professional bio, and positioning |
| Ⅲ — Curation | What belongs, quality over quantity, and the master portfolio |
| Ⅳ — Case Studies | How to document individual projects for any discipline |
| Ⅴ — By Discipline | Specific guidance for CS/IT, Engineering, Design, Research, Education, Business, and more |
| Ⅵ — Entry Points | Starting fresh, final year, postgraduate, and building quickly from existing work |
| Ⅶ — Platforms | GitHub, personal websites, Behance, LinkedIn, and print |
| Ⅷ — Keeping It Alive | The portfolio as a living system — not a one-time submission |

---

## Who This Is For

Itan is written for:

- undergraduate students at any stage of their degree / qualification
- final-year students approaching graduation and professional entry
- postgraduate students making research work visible beyond academia
- professionals building or rebuilding a portfolio at any career stage

It is designed to work across disciplines. The philosophy is universal.
The guidance is specific where it needs to be.
